🤖Vivi's assigment of projection mapping workshop

In my 3D scanning park project, I employed particleization techniques to bring the flowers to life. Through meticulous 3D scanning, I captured the essence of these flowers, ensuring their intricate details were faithfully represented in digital form. Utilizing TouchDesigner, I then applied mesmerizing particle effects inspired by musical visualizations to imbue the flowers with dynamic energy.

The result was a captivating fusion of art and technology, as the flowers' digital counterparts danced gracefully across different spaces within the room. Each particle effect was meticulously choreographed to interact harmoniously with the accompanying music, creating a multisensory experience that engaged both sight and sound.Throughout this assignment, I delved deep into the realms of 3D scanning techniques and TouchDesigner particle effects, honing my skills in digital artistry and audiovisual integration. This journey not only expanded my technical knowledge but also deepened my appreciation for the boundless creativity that emerges at the intersection of technology and art.

Picture 1 shows the particleized video output by Touch designer. I first drag the 3D scan file into Touch designer, use the component pointtransform to build the point cloud particleization of the scan file, use the thresh component to build different color thresholds to increase the softness of the particle diffusion, add the feedback component to make the particles have a shadow effect, and finally add the rendering three-piece suite: render component / geometry/camera.

Next, I select the point cloud system through pointselect, connect to math to change the range of values, connect to text3D to build the point cloud texture and color, and finally drag in the geometry's instancing color OP and select RGB respectively. the color of the point cloud is set up!

Picture 2 illustrates my specialization in learning how TouchDesigner facilitates the interaction between music and images. In Figure 3, I have documented my notes in Chinese, outlining the process as follows: Firstly, I utilize the 'audiofilein' to import the created music file into TouchDesigner. Next, I analyze various music parameters using 'audioAnalysis,' such as high-level and low-level features. I then select a specific channel and adjust its value range using the 'math' component. Additionally, I utilize the 'speed' component to control the frequency of sound changes, which subsequently informs visual alterations based on this frequency. To activate the component, I connect it to the 'null' component and adjust the parameters within the parent set.
